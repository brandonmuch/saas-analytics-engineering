Monthly Recurring Revenue (often abbreviated as MRR) is the predictable, recurring income a subscription business earns each month from its active customers.

It is calculated by summing the monthly subscription value of every currently active customer. For example, if a business has 100 customers paying $99 per month, its Monthly Recurring Revenue is $9,900.

In this project, Monthly Recurring Revenue is broken down into four components each month:
- **New**: revenue from customers who signed up during that month.
- **Expansion**: additional revenue from existing customers who upgraded to a more expensive plan.
- **Contraction**: revenue lost from existing customers who downgraded to a cheaper plan.
- **Churned**: revenue lost from customers who cancelled entirely.

This breakdown is commonly referred to as the "MRR waterfall," since it shows how revenue flows up and down between periods rather than only reporting a single total figure.
